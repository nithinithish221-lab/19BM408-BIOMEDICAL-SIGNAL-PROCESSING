# THEORY :
Electroencephalography (EEG) is a non-invasive technique used to record the electrical activity of the brain using electrodes placed on the scalp. The recorded signals represent the collective electrical activity generated by millions of neurons in the cerebral cortex. EEG signals are typically measured in microvolts and are characterized by different rhythmic patterns that correspond to various brain states.
EEG signals are classified into different frequency bands:

•	Delta waves (0.5–4 Hz) – associated with deep sleep

•	Theta waves (4–8 Hz) – associated with drowsiness and meditation

•	Alpha waves (8–13 Hz) – observed when a person is relaxed with eyes closed

•	Beta waves (13–30 Hz) – associated with active thinking and concentration

•	Gamma waves (>30 Hz) – related to higher cognitive functions

EEG signal analysis is widely used in clinical diagnosis and neuroscience research. It plays an important role in detecting neurological disorders such as epilepsy, sleep disorders, brain tumors, and encephalopathy.
Biomedical engineers use signal processing techniques to analyze EEG signals in both time domain and frequency domain. In time-domain analysis, the waveform of the EEG signal is observed to understand the variation in amplitude with respect to time.
MATLAB provides powerful tools for biomedical signal processing, allowing visualization, filtering, and analysis of EEG signals efficiently.
